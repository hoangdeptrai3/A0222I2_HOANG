We use still as an adverb to emphasise that something is continuing: They have been together for 40 years and they are still very much in love. We’re still waiting for our new couch to be delivered. We usually put still in the normal mid position for adverbs (between the subject and the main verb, or after the modal verb or first auxiliary verb, or after be as a main verb): She still goes to French classes every week. (between subject and main verb) He’s still studying. (after the modal verb or first auxiliary verb) I’m still hungry. (after main verb be)
